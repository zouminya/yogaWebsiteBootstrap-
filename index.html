Yoga Helps With Pain Relief

Yoga has been showed and approved to help people with a range of chronic pain conditions. Practicing yoga can stretch and strengthen muscles. It helps reduce the muscular tension, build flexibility, mobility and strength, ease pain and improve balance. It is important to focus on breathing while practice yoga as well.

Breathing Techniques:

Ocean’s Breathing

  1. Inhale deeply through your nose, exhale slowly through your mouth.
  2. Make a “HA” sound as you exhale slowly.
  3. Focus on your breath
  4. Repeat it

Alternate Nostril Breathing

  1. Lift your right hand toward to your face
  2. Use the thumb to press and close your right nostril, inhale deeply through your leftnostril.
  3. Use your index figure, lose your left nostril, release your thumb, and exhale through your right nostril.
  4. Inhale through the right nostril, close the right nostril.
  5. Open the left nostril and exhale through the left nostril
  6. Repeat the cycle.

Box Breathing

  1. Slowly exhale through your mouth, count to four.
  2. Hold your breath for four seconds.
  3. Deeply inhale through your nose, count to four.
  4. Hold your breath for four seconds.
  5. Repeat the cycle.

Disclaimer:

Yoga is one of many ways that can help with pain. However, it might not work for everyone. It is best to follow your doctor’s advises. If you are new to yoga and want to try yoga, try to take the in-person instructed classes. Yoga poses can be modified according to your strength, experience and condition. Keep in mind, never push yourself further into any poses than what feels comfortable. You can get hurt. Flexibility and strength will come but it takes time.
